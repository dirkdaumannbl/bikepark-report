Trails
Character
One of Germany's most versatile parks: easier flow trails on the chairlift (right) side, heavier freeride/downhill terrain on the gondola (left) side. Trails laid out by leading designer Diddie Schneider. [2][5]
Trail types
Flow, flow country, pump track/trail, freeride, four-cross (4X), enduro (natural/rooty), downhill, jump line [6][4]
Difficulty spread
4312 — 10 trails total, 15.6 km (operator grades: 4 easy/green, 3 medium/blue, 1 hard/red enduro, 2 hard/black downhill & jump) [6]
Vertical drop
~250 m on the Ettelsberg [5]
Longest descent
Flow trails ~2,900 m each; Downhill ~1,600 m [5]

Features & suitability
Family friendliness
Good — multiple flow trails and a pump track; the first trails are all flow with optional features [7]
Beginner-friendliness
High — flow & flow-country lines plus a training parcours for progression [6]
Skills area
Yes (no belt) — Training Parcours + pump track/trail; no magic-carpet/conveyor mentioned [6][5]
Standout features
2005/2006 World-Cup downhill (rocky/rooty, road gap and up-to-6 m drop); "Jump or Leave" jump line; four-cross track [8][5]

Practical
Bike shop / wash
On-site bike shop, rental and workshop; bike wash available [5]
Camper van / camping
Not directly at the park; Wohnmobilpark Willingen (camper spots at the foot of the Ettelsberg) and Campingplatz Bahrenberg nearby [9][5]
Reputation / reviews
Held in the same high regard as Winterberg; praised as versatile, often quieter with fewer queues; hosts a major BIKE Festival [8][7]

Trails
Character
"The Mother" — Germany's original and most versatile bike park; a classic, welcoming all-rounder with a clear difficulty structure from kids' parcours to World-Cup downhill. [6][2]
Trail types
Downhill, freeride, flow/flow-country, jump lines, north shore, enduro triple, slopestyle/freestyle area [7]
Difficulty spread
3562 — ~17–18 trails (operator levels: very easy / easy / medium / difficult), plus unclassified expert jump lines [7]
Vertical drop
Not found (full-park figure not published; iXS Downhill ~116 m / 381 ft per trail database) [8]
Longest descent
SRAM Flow Country is the longest trail [8]

Features & suitability
Family friendliness
Very high — broad graded selection; "a park for everyone, warm and welcoming, like family" [6]
Beginner-friendliness
High — free Beginner & Kids Parcours accessible without a ticket [4]
Skills area
Yes (no belt) — Beginner & Kids Parcours and a pump-track practice course; the Schneewittchen lift has a conveyor entry but the skills area itself is not a magic-carpet zone [4]
Standout features
iXS Downhill race track; Slopestyle area — one of few in Europe with big jumps "for everybody"; Dirt Masters festival venue [6][7]

Practical
Bike shop / wash
Bikestation: rental (Ghost), repairs, parts (SRAM, RockShox, Schwalbe); 10 bike-wash spots across three locations [4]
Camper van / camping
On-lot overnight/wild camping prohibited; Hostel Erlebnisberg Kappe adjacent, Campingplatz / Wohnmobilpark Winterberg nearby (e.g. 75 pitches) [4][9]
Reputation / reviews
Regarded as a "must visit" classic; consistently praised for trail variety and family appeal; can get busy/crowded on peak days [6][7]

Lift type / capacity
Two parallel drag/T-bar lifts with "EasyLoop" system (clips behind the stem); typical wait 5–10 min. Capacity (persons/hour) Not found [4][5]

Roughly March–November, weather-dependent; operating days announced on the site [1]

Fri–Sun 10:00–18:00; Thu 15:00–18:00 (Jun–Aug); occasional Mon–Wed pop-up rides [5]
Gravity Card
No — not listed in the Gravity Card network [6]

Trails
Character
Opened 2022; "little Canada" in the Sauerland — Canadian/Whistler-style trails (piste-bully, big wooden bridges, oversized logo signs), very flowy and well shaped, with standout jump lines. [2][4]
Trail types
Flow trails, technical sections, jump lines (flow vs. technical split per grade) [2]
Difficulty spread
1336 — 14 trails total (1 green, 3 blue, 3 red, 6 black) [2][4]
Vertical drop
~200 m (steep parallel drag lifts) [2]
Longest descent
Not found

Features & suitability
Family friendliness
Good — coaching programs, kids camps; green/blue progression suits families [7]
Beginner-friendliness
High — described as an "absolute stroke of luck" for beginners; flowy green line + progressive blues with a valley practice course [5][7]
Skills area
Yes (no belt) — beginner practice course in the valley; no magic-carpet/conveyor mentioned [7][4]
Standout features
Excellent jump lines; Canadian-style wooden structures; snowcat/slopestyle drop features; hosts BYND Festival and "King of Groomer" race series [4][7]

Practical
Bike shop / wash
Bike shop with rentals (Rocky Mountain) & protective gear (POC); multiple bike-wash stations in the upper car park (water runs ~2 h after lift close) [7]
Camper van / camping
Limited camper/motorhome spaces (no advance booking) in the lower car park; new 24-h sanitary block with coin showers (€2); camping ~€15/night [7][5]
Reputation / reviews
Highly positive — praised for diverse, well-built, flowy trails, friendly staff and good food; widely covered as a top new German park [4][2]

Character
A long-established Bavarian-Forest classic with huge variety, "from perfectly shaped flow trails through technical enduro tracks to classic downhill," plus an uphill-flow trail for e-MTB. [2]
Trail types
Flow / flow-country, enduro (red & black), freeride (red & black), jump line, downhill, pump track, uphill-flow [2][3]
Difficulty spread
584 — 5 easy/green (4.1 km), 8 intermediate/blue (9.4 km), 4 difficult/red (3.9 km), plus black expert variants (Evil Eye 1.0/2.0, Downhill) [3][2]
Vertical drop
260 m (mountain station to valley) [2][3]
Longest descent
Downhill track ~1.9 km over 260 vertical metres [3]

Features & suitability
Family friendliness
Good — Flow Country Trail designed so beginners and families can ride it; separate kids' course [2]
Beginner-friendliness
High — two blue flow trails plus a practice course; on-site bike school (Rock my Trail) [2]
Skills area
Yes (no belt) — Übungsparcours with tables, doubles & an airbag landing, plus a kids' course; no magic-carpet/conveyor mentioned [2][3]
Standout features
Airbag jump, drop battery, tableline, pump track and the e-MTB "Uphill Flow" trail; long downhill/freeride pedigree [2]

Practical
Bike shop / wash
On-site bike shop, rental and workshop; bike wash available [2]
Camper van / camping
Camper-van parking with electricity on site; overnight at the Geißkopf car park referenced by the operator [2][4]
Reputation / reviews
Long-regarded as one of Germany's best-known parks for variety and trail quality; restaurant and bike school on site [2][6]

May – October [3]
Operating hours
Not found (chairlift page lists status only, not daily hours) [6]
Gravity Card
Yes — member since the 2024 season [4]

Trails
Character
Speed-focused park with some of the longest continuous flow descents in Europe; trails graded into 4 difficulty levels over 30+ km. [1][3]
Trail types
Flow line, enduro, downhill, singletrack, pump track; plus enduro race special-stages [4][2]
Difficulty spread
1112 — 5 marked trails across 4 levels: Azur (easy/green-blue), Rubin (blue-red flow), Baron (red-black), Illegal & Downhill (black/expert) [2][3]
Vertical drop
Up to ~481 m (Klínovec summit to base) [5]
Longest descent
Azur ~10.3 km; Rubin ~8 km; Baron ~6 km — among Europe's longest continuous descents [2][3]

Features & suitability
Family friendliness
Moderate — long easy flow lines (Azur) suit families, but the park is best known for speed; chairlift also carries scooters/strollers [3][2]
Beginner-friendliness
Moderate — reviewers note long flowy descents are accessible, though it leans toward speed/downhill riders rather than first-timers [2]
Skills area
Yes (no belt) — skill zone at the upper lift station with a pump track, wooden footbridges and two drops; no magic-carpet/conveyor mentioned [2]
Standout features
"Illegal" natural trail (one of Europe's most renowned); 30-year-tradition downhill; Baron tabletops up to 4 m and rock gardens [4][2]

Practical
Bike shop / wash
Bike rental, sales and repair (SKI KLÍNOVEC s.r.o.); bike & scooter wash; showers on site [3]
Camper van / camping
Motorhome parking and accommodation referenced at the resort [3][4]
Reputation / reviews
Positive for speed/flow fans; trails reported in very good condition and a notably long season; some reviewers feel grades run a touch high [2]
